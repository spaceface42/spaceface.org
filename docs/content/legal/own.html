Website analytics Our website uses analytics to help us better understand visitor traffic, so we can improve our services. Our data is anonymous and it is impossible that under any circumstances, we may connect it to you. ​ Collection of Non-Personal Data When you visit our website, we use a privacy-first analytics platform, to collect non-personal data about your visit. Our analytics is designed to respect the privacy of our website visitors by not collecting any personal data or personally identifiable information (PII) and does not use cookies. The non-personal data collected by our analytics may include: ​ Your IP address (anonymised): Our analytics collects the IP address of the devices accessing our website. However, the IP addresses are immediately anonymised, meaning that the last octet of the IP address is removed, making it impossible to identify individual visitors. Anonymised IP addresses are used for statistical analysis and are not linked to any other data. The type of device and browser you are using: Our analytics collects information about the type of device (e.g., desktop, mobile) and the web browser (e.g., Chrome, Safari) used by visitors. This helps us understand the technology preferences of our audience and optimise our website accordingly. This information is collected in an aggregated manner and does not identify individual users. The pages you visit on our website: Our analytics tracks the pages you visit on our website, providing us with insights into which content is most popular and helping us improve our website structure and navigation. This data is collected in an aggregated format and does not identify individual users. The time and duration of your visit: Our analytics records the time of your visit and the duration of your session on our website. This information allows us to analyse user engagement patterns and make informed decisions about website improvements. This data is collected in an aggregated format and does not identify individual users. Referral information: Our analytics captures referral information, which means it records the website or source that led you to our website. This information helps us understand our marketing and advertising efforts' effectiveness and identify which channels drive traffic to our website. Referral information is collected in an aggregated format and does not contain any personally identifiable information. ​ Purpose of Data Collection We collect non-personal data to understand how visitors use our website, improve our content and user experience, and make informed decisions to enhance our services. This data helps us analyse trends, track user engagement, and measure the effectiveness of our marketing campaigns.
